When most people think of the Internet they will assume you are talking about websites, video streaming services, music radios and social media forums. The idea of an internetwork of computers was the vision of a man named J.C. Licklider. He envision a network of computers that can communicate to each no matter where it is located or who manufactured the machine. He created the Intergalatic Computer Network, which at the time connected computers over existing POTS lines then was eventually upgrade to coaxial cable.
ARPANet was the evolution to thet Intergalatic Computer Network and allowed the government enitity know as ARAP (Advanced Research Projects Agency) to work on network computers that were located throughout the country. Not only did ARPANet provide network communication it also provided a robust communication outlet in the event that the nation was attacked data communication between all computers was not halted.

The Internet was later opened to the public by Congress in 1992.

The World Wide Web begins and starts to gain popular during its early years.

Today the Internet is used by a billion plus people a day
